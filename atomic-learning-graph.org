* Learning Path

Follow these concepts in prerequisite order. Every concept and lesson step carries a verbatim source receipt.

Goal: [[id:self-attention][Self-Attention]]

1. [[id:vectors][Vectors]] — A vector is a fixed-length array whose entries are scalars.

2. [[id:dot-product][Dot Product]] — The dot product sums products of corresponding elements from two vectors.

3. [[id:matrix-vector-product][Matrix–Vector Product]] — A matrix–vector product produces a vector whose entries are dot products between matrix rows and the input vector.

4. [[id:softmax][Softmax]] — Softmax exponentiates a collection of outputs and normalizes them into values that sum to one.

5. [[id:qkv][Queries, Keys, and Values]] — An attention database can be represented as key–value pairs that are accessed using a query.

6. [[id:attention-pooling][Attention Pooling]] — Attention pooling combines values linearly using weights determined by query–key compatibility.

7. [[id:self-attention][Self-Attention]] — Self-attention lets every token attend to the other tokens in the same sequence.

8. [[id:positional-encoding][Positional Encoding]] — Positional encodings provide token-order information to models as additional token-associated inputs.

9. [[id:softmax-ordering][Softmax Preserves Ordering]] — Softmax preserves the ordering of its inputs, so the largest logit identifies the highest-probability class.

10. [[id:vector-norm][Vector Norm]] — A vector norm expresses the magnitude or size of a vector.

* Dot Product
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: dot-product
:ROAM_REFS: @d2l-linear-algebra-dot-product
:END:

The dot product sums products of corresponding elements from two vectors.

** Prerequisites
- [[id:vectors]]

** Source

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

URL: https://github.com/d2l-ai/d2l-en/blob/b2e2ae30898a9d0126a9699ae7e441de3e272715/chapter_preliminaries/linear-algebra.md

#+begin_quote
Given two vectors $\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} \in \mathbb{R}^d$, their dot product $\mathbf{x}^\top \mathbf{y}$ (also known as inner product, $\langle \mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}  \rangle$) is a sum over the products of the elements at the same position: $\mathbf{x}^\top \mathbf{y} = \sum_{i=1}^{d} x_i y_i$.
#+end_quote

** Lesson: Multiply Matching Entries, Then Add

*** Step 1 (core)

The dot product of two vectors is found by multiplying entries in the same position and adding those products.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
The dot product of two vectors is a sum over the products of the elements at the same position
#+end_quote

*** Step 2 (deep)

A weighted sum can be written as the dot product of a vector of values and a vector of weights.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
the weighted sum of the values in $\mathbf{x}$ according to the weights $\mathbf{w}$ could be expressed as the dot product $\mathbf{x}^\top \mathbf{w}$.
#+end_quote

*** Step 3 (deep)

If the weights are nonnegative and add up to 1, the dot product gives a weighted average.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
When the weights are nonnegative and sum to $1$, i.e., $\left(\sum_{i=1}^{n} {w_i} = 1\right)$, the dot product expresses a weighted average.
#+end_quote

* Matrix–Vector Product
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: matrix-vector-product
:ROAM_REFS: @d2l-linear-algebra-matrix-vector-product
:END:

A matrix–vector product produces a vector whose entries are dot products between matrix rows and the input vector.

** Prerequisites
- [[id:dot-product]]

** Source

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

URL: https://github.com/d2l-ai/d2l-en/blob/b2e2ae30898a9d0126a9699ae7e441de3e272715/chapter_preliminaries/linear-algebra.md

#+begin_quote
The matrix--vector product $\mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}$ is simply a column vector of length $m$, whose $i^\textrm{th}$ element is the dot product $\mathbf{a}^\top_i \mathbf{x}$: $$
\mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}
= \begin{bmatrix}
\mathbf{a}^\top_{1} \\
\mathbf{a}^\top_{2} \\
\vdots \\
\mathbf{a}^\top_m \\
\end{bmatrix}\mathbf{x}
= \begin{bmatrix}
 \mathbf{a}^\top_{1} \mathbf{x}  \\
 \mathbf{a}^\top_{2} \mathbf{x} \\
\vdots\\
 \mathbf{a}^\top_{m} \mathbf{x}\\
\end{bmatrix}.
$$
#+end_quote

** Lesson: Multiplying a Matrix by a Vector

*** Step 1 (core)

Multiplying matrix A by vector x produces a column vector of length m. Each entry is the dot product of one row of A with x.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
The matrix--vector product $\mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}$ is simply a column vector of length $m$, whose $i^\textrm{th}$ element is the dot product $\mathbf{a}^\top_i \mathbf{x}$
#+end_quote

*** Step 2 (core)

The number of columns in A must equal the length of x.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
Note that the column dimension of A (its length along axis 1) must be the same as the dimension of x (its length).
#+end_quote

*** Step 3 (deep)

Multiplying by a matrix can be viewed as a transformation that maps a vector with n entries to one with m entries.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
We can think of multiplication with a matrix $\mathbf{A}\in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ as a transformation that projects vectors from $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ to $\mathbb{R}^{m}$.
#+end_quote

* Softmax
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: softmax
:ROAM_REFS: @d2l-softmax-regression-softmax
:END:

Softmax exponentiates a collection of outputs and normalizes them into values that sum to one.

** Prerequisites
- [[id:dot-product]]

** Source

Source: d2l-softmax-regression

URL: https://github.com/d2l-ai/d2l-en/blob/b2e2ae30898a9d0126a9699ae7e441de3e272715/chapter_linear-classification/softmax-regression.md

#+begin_quote
Putting these two pieces together gives us the softmax function: $$\hat{\mathbf{y}} = \mathrm{softmax}(\mathbf{o}) \quad \textrm{where}\quad \hat{y}_i = \frac{\exp(o_i)}{\sum_j \exp(o_j)}.$$
#+end_quote

** Lesson: Turning scores into normalized values

*** Step 1 (core)

Softmax exponentiates each input, then divides it by the sum of all the exponentiated inputs.

Source: d2l-softmax-regression

#+begin_quote
Putting these two pieces together gives us the softmax function: $$\hat{\mathbf{y}} = \mathrm{softmax}(\mathbf{o}) \quad \textrm{where}\quad \hat{y}_i = \frac{\exp(o_i)}{\sum_j \exp(o_j)}.$$
#+end_quote

*** Step 2 (deep)

The largest input corresponds to the class that softmax rates as most likely.

Source: d2l-softmax-regression

#+begin_quote
Note that the largest coordinate of $\mathbf{o}$ corresponds to the most likely class according to $\hat{\mathbf{y}}$.
#+end_quote

*** Step 1 (core)

Attention pooling makes a linear combination of values. Its weights come from how compatible the query is with each key.

Source: d2l-queries-keys-values
