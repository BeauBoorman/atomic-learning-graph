* Learning Path

Follow these concepts in prerequisite order. Every concept and lesson step carries a verbatim source receipt.

Goal: [[id:self-attention][Self-Attention]]

1. [[id:vectors][Vectors]] — A vector is a fixed-length array whose elements are scalars.

2. [[id:dot-product][Dot Product]] — The dot product of two vectors sums the products of elements at corresponding positions.

3. [[id:matrix-vector-product][Matrix–Vector Product]] — A matrix–vector product contains one dot product between the input vector and each row of the matrix.

4. [[id:softmax][Softmax]] — Softmax exponentiates scores and normalizes them into values that sum to one.

5. [[id:qkv][Queries, Keys, and Values]] — Attention can be framed using a query applied to a collection of key–value pairs.

6. [[id:attention-pooling][Attention Pooling]] — Attention pooling combines values linearly using weights determined by query–key compatibility.

7. [[id:self-attention][Self-Attention]] — Self-attention lets every token attend to every other token in the same sequence.

8. [[id:positional-encoding][Positional Encoding]] — Positional encodings provide token-order information to models as additional token-associated inputs.

9. [[id:softmax-ordering][Softmax Preserves Ordering]] — Softmax preserves score ordering, so the largest input score identifies the class with the highest output probability.

10. [[id:vector-norm][Vector Norm]] — A vector norm provides a scalar measure of the vector's magnitude.

* Dot Product
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: dot-product
:ROAM_REFS: https://github.com/d2l-ai/d2l-en/blob/b2e2ae30898a9d0126a9699ae7e441de3e272715/chapter_preliminaries/linear-algebra.md#dot-product

The dot product of two vectors sums the products of elements at corresponding positions.

** Prerequisites
- [[id:vectors]]

** Source

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

URL: https://github.com/d2l-ai/d2l-en/blob/b2e2ae30898a9d0126a9699ae7e441de3e272715/chapter_preliminaries/linear-algebra.md

#+begin_quote
Given two vectors $\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} \in \mathbb{R}^d$, their dot product $\mathbf{x}^\top \mathbf{y}$ (also known as inner product, $\langle \mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}  \rangle$) is a sum over the products of the elements at the same position: $\mathbf{x}^\top \mathbf{y} = \sum_{i=1}^{d} x_i y_i$.
#+end_quote

** Lesson: Multiply Matching Numbers, Then Add

*** Step 1 (core)

For two vectors—ordered lists of numbers—the dot product multiplies numbers in matching positions and adds those products together.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
The dot product of two vectors is a sum over the products of the elements at the same position
#+end_quote

*** Step 2 (deep)

A dot product can represent a weighted sum, where each value is multiplied by its assigned weight.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
the weighted sum of the values in $\mathbf{x}$ according to the weights $\mathbf{w}$ could be expressed as the dot product $\mathbf{x}^\top \mathbf{w}$.
#+end_quote

*** Step 3 (deep)

If the weights are nonnegative and add up to 1, the dot product represents a weighted average.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
When the weights are nonnegative and sum to $1$, i.e., $\left(\sum_{i=1}^{n} {w_i} = 1\right)$, the dot product expresses a weighted average.
#+end_quote

* Matrix–Vector Product
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: matrix-vector-product
:ROAM_REFS: https://github.com/d2l-ai/d2l-en/blob/b2e2ae30898a9d0126a9699ae7e441de3e272715/chapter_preliminaries/linear-algebra.md#matrix-vector-product
:END:

A matrix–vector product contains one dot product between the input vector and each row of the matrix.

** Prerequisites
- [[id:dot-product]]

** Source

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

URL: https://github.com/d2l-ai/d2l-en/blob/b2e2ae30898a9d0126a9699ae7e441de3e272715/chapter_preliminaries/linear-algebra.md

#+begin_quote
The matrix--vector product $\mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}$ is simply a column vector of length $m$, whose $i^\textrm{th}$ element is the dot product $\mathbf{a}^\top_i \mathbf{x}$: $$
\mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}
= \begin{bmatrix}
\mathbf{a}^\top_{1} \\
\mathbf{a}^\top_{2} \\
\vdots \\
\mathbf{a}^\top_m \\
\end{bmatrix}\mathbf{x}
= \begin{bmatrix}
 \mathbf{a}^\top_{1} \mathbf{x}  \\
 \mathbf{a}^\top_{2} \mathbf{x} \\
\vdots\\
 \mathbf{a}^\top_{m} \mathbf{x}\\
\end{bmatrix}.
$$
#+end_quote

** Lesson: Multiplying a Matrix by a Vector

*** Step 1 (core)

The result of multiplying matrix A by vector x is a column vector with m entries.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
The matrix--vector product $\mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}$ is simply a column vector of length $m$
#+end_quote

*** Step 2 (core)

Each entry in the result is the dot product—the sum of paired values multiplied together—of x and the matching row of A.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
whose $i^\textrm{th}$ element is the dot product $\mathbf{a}^\top_i \mathbf{x}$
#+end_quote

*** Step 3 (core)

For the multiplication to work, the number of columns in A must equal the number of entries in x.

Source: d2l-linear-algebra

#+begin_quote
Note that the column dimension of A (its length along axis 1) must be the same as the dimension of x (its length).
#+end_quote

:ID: softmax

Softmax exponentiates scores and normalizes them into values that sum to one.

** Prerequisites
- [[id:dot-product]]

** Source

Source: d2l-softmax-regression

URL: https://github.com/d2l-ai/d2l-en/blob/b2e2ae30898a9d0126a9699ae7e441de3e272715/chapter_linear-classification/softmax-regression.md

#+begin_quote
We can then transform these values so that they add up to $1$ by dividing each by their sum.
#+end_quote

** Lesson: Turning scores into values that add up to one

*** Step 1 (core)

Softmax first applies the exponential function to each score, then divides each result by the sum of all the exponentiated scores.

Source: d2l-softmax-regression

#+begin_quote
Putting these two pieces together gives us the softmax function: $$\hat{\mathbf{y}} = \mathrm{softmax}(\mathbf{o}) \quad \textrm{where}\quad \hat{y}_i = \frac{\exp(o_i)}{\sum_j \exp(o_j)}.$$
#+end_quote

*** Step 2 (core)

Dividing each value by their sum makes the resulting values add up to 1. This is called normalization.

Source: d2l-softmax-regression

#+begin_quote
We can then transform these values so that they add up to $1$ by dividing each by their sum. This process is called normalization.
#+end_quote

*** Step 3 (deep)

The largest original score corresponds to the class that softmax considers most likely.

Source: d2l-softmax-regression

#+begin_quote
Note that the largest coordinate of $\mathbf{o}$ corresponds to the most likely class according to $\hat{\mathbf{y}}$.
#+end_quote

* Queries, Keys, and Values
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: qkv
:ROAM_REFS: https://github.com/d2l-ai/d2l-en/blob/b2e2ae30898a9d0126a9699ae7e441de3e272715/chapter_attention-mechanisms-and-transformers/queries-keys-values.md#qkv
:END:

Attention can be framed using a query applied to a collection of key–value pairs.

** Prerequisites
- [[id:softmax]]

** Source

Source: d2l-queries-keys-values

URL: https://github.com/d2l-ai/d2l-en/blob/b2e2ae30898a9d0126a9699ae7e441de3e272715/chapter_attention-mechanisms-and-transformers/queries-keys-values.md

#+begin_quote
For now, simply consider the following: denote by $\mathcal{D} \stackrel{\textrm{def}}{=} \{(\mathbf{k}_1, \mathbf{v}_1), \ldots (\mathbf{k}_m, \mathbf{v}_m)\}$ a database of $m$ tuples of keys and values.
#+end_quote

** Lesson: How attention uses queries, keys, and values

*** Step 1 (core)

Think of the data as a collection of key–value pairs, and call the item used to search or compare against them the query.

Source: d2l-queries-keys-values

#+begin_quote
denote by $\mathcal{D} \stackrel{\textrm{def}}{=} \{(\mathbf{k}_1, \mathbf{v}_1), \ldots (\mathbf{k}_m, \mathbf{v}_m)\}$ a database of $m$ tuples of keys and values. Moreover, denote by $\mathbf{q}$ a query.
#+end_quote

*** Step 2 (core)

Attention gives each value a numeric weight based on its key and the query, then adds the weighted values together.

Source: d2l-queries-keys-values

#+begin_quote
Then we can define the attention over $\mathcal{D}$ as $$\textrm{Attention}(\mathbf{q}, \mathcal{D}) \stackrel{\textrm{def}}{=} \sum_{i=1}^m \alpha(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{k}_i) \mathbf{v}_i,$$ where $\alpha(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{k}_i) \in \mathbb{R}$ ($i = 1, \ldots, m$) are scalar attention weights.
#+end_quote

*** Step 3 (core)

A value receives more attention when its weight is large.

Source: d2l-queries-keys-values

#+begin_quote
The name attention derives from the fact that the operation pays particular attention to the terms for which the weight $\alpha$ is significant (i.e., large).
#+end_quote

*** Step 4 (deep)

In the most common deep-learning setup, every weight is nonnegative and all the weights add up to 1.

Source: d2l-queries-keys-values

#+begin_quote
The weights $\alpha(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{k}_i)$ form a convex combination, i.e., $\sum_i \alpha(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{k}_i) = 1$ and $\alpha(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{k}_i) \geq 0$ for all $i$. This is the most common setting in deep learning.
#+end_quote

Source: d2l-queries-keys-values

** Lesson: Combining Values by Relevance

*** Step 1 (core)

Attention pooling makes a weighted linear combination of values. Each weight comes from how compatible the query is with a key.

Source: d2l-queries-keys-values

#+begin_quote
The attention mechanism computes a linear combination over values $\mathbf{v}_\mathit{i}$ via attention pooling, where weights are derived according to the compatibility between a query $\mathbf{q}$ and keys $\mathbf{k}_\mathit{i}$.
#+end_quote

*** Step 2 (deep)

When the weights are nonnegative and add up to 1, large weights can be viewed as the model selecting relevant parts—but this is only an intuition.

Source: d2l-queries-keys-values

#+begin_quote
One of the benefits of the attention mechanism is that it can be quite intuitive, particularly when the weights are nonnegative and sum to $1$. In this case we might interpret large weights as a way for the model to select components of relevance. While this is a good intuition, it is important to remember that it is just that, an intuition.
#+end_quote
